What is Peripheral Vascular Disease?

Peripheral Vascular Disease, or PVD, is a common circulation problem that affects millions of Americans every year.**

What are the symptoms of PVD?

  • Discomfort or pain in one or both legs

  • Skin on the lower legs may be cool to touch, thin or shining

  • Lower extremity fatigue

  • Pain such as burning and aching at rest, commonly in the toes and at night while lying flat

  • Erectile dysfunction (ED) or impotence

Your chances of having PVD are higher if...

  • You're 50 or older

  • You're African American

  • You smoke or have smoked

  • You have diabetes, high blood pressure or high blood cholesterol

  • You have a personal or family history of heart disease, heart attack or stroke

What happens if PVD goes untreated?

Left untreated, PVD can lead to gangrene, amputation of limbs, and death from heart attack or stroke.
